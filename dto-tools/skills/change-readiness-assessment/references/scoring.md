# Scoring and interpretation

## Point values

- "Sí" = 2 points
- "Parcial" = 1 point
- "No" = 0 points

## Max score per dimension

| Dimensión | # preguntas | Puntaje máximo |
|---|---|---|
| Awareness | 6 | 12 |
| Desire | 5 | 10 |
| Knowledge | 6 | 12 |
| Ability | 4 | 8 |
| Reinforcement | 3 | 6 |
| **Total** | **24** | **48** |

## Percentage and readiness level

For each dimension and for the total, compute `score / max * 100` rounded to the nearest whole percent. Classify using:

| % | Nivel |
|---|---|
| ≥ 80% | Alta preparación |
| 50–79% | Preparación moderada — hay riesgos que gestionar |
| < 50% | Baja preparación — riesgo alto |

## The ADKAR barrier point (the key consultant insight)

ADKAR is sequential — Awareness enables Desire, Desire enables the effort to build Knowledge, Knowledge enables Ability, Ability sustains Reinforcement. A team can score high on later dimensions and it still won't hold if an earlier one is weak, because the later scores are often optimistic/theoretical rather than durable.

To find the **barrier point** (the dimension the change effort should focus on first): go through the five dimensions **in ADKAR order** (Awareness → Desire → Knowledge → Ability → Reinforcement) and find the first one scoring below 60%. That's the barrier point — report it explicitly and center the recommendations there, even if a later dimension scored lower in raw percentage. If all five are ≥60%, there's no barrier point; note the lowest-scoring dimension as the area to keep strengthening instead.

## What goes in the PDF

- A results table: dimension, score/max, percentage, level.
- A simple horizontal bar visual per dimension (percentage-filled bar), so the gaps are visible at a glance.
- The overall total score/percentage and readiness level.
- The identified barrier point (or, if none, the lowest-scoring dimension) with a short consultant-style paragraph explaining why that's the priority and what addressing it typically requires (e.g. a Knowledge gap usually means the plan needs more training/enablement before pushing further; an Ability gap usually means tools/process/time constraints need solving, not more communication).
- An "Acciones sugeridas" section with concrete recommended actions for the barrier point and every other dimension below 80% — see `recommendations.md`.
- Every individual question with its answer (Sí/Parcial/No) and any follow-up note captured for "No" answers.
