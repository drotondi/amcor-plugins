# Change Coach — ADKAR & Kotter Playbook

Reference knowledge base for the `change-coach` skill. Read this once per session and keep it in context — don't re-read it mid-conversation.

## How the two models relate

- **ADKAR (Prosci)** operates at the **individual/team level**: it explains why *one person or one group* does or doesn't adopt a change. Use it when the question is about resistance, adoption, a specific team's or stakeholder's readiness, or "why isn't this person/team changing behavior".
- **Kotter's 8-Step Process** operates at the **organizational/initiative level**: it explains how to *lead the overall change effort* from kickoff to institutionalization. Use it when the question is about how to sequence, sponsor, communicate, or sustain a broader change initiative.
- They are complementary, not competing: a Kotter step (e.g. Step 5 "Enable action") often fails because of an ADKAR gap (e.g. Ability) in the people affected by it. When a query spans both, name which Kotter step is in play and which ADKAR dimension explains why the people in it are stuck.

## ADKAR (Prosci)

| Dimension | What it means | Typical blocker | Pragmatic diagnostic question |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Awareness** | The person understands *why* the change is happening and the risk of not changing. | Comms only explained the "what", never the "why now" or the cost of inaction. | "¿La persona podría explicar en sus propias palabras por qué se hace este cambio, sin citar el memo oficial?" |
| **Desire** | The person is personally motivated to support and participate in the change (not just informed). | Change is seen as something done *to* them, not *with* them; no clear "what's in it for me"; distrust in leadership or past change fatigue. | "¿Qué gana o qué pierde esta persona/equipo específicamente con este cambio?" |
| **Knowledge** | The person knows *how* to change — the new process, tools, skills, behaviors. | Training was generic, one-off, or happened too early/late relative to go-live. | "¿Recibió entrenamiento específico a su rol, y lo recibió lo suficientemente cerca del momento en que lo necesita?" |
| **Ability** | The person can actually *demonstrate* the new skills/behaviors in real conditions, not just in training. | Gap between "trained" and "capable under real workload/pressure"; no practice time, no coaching on the job, systems/tools not actually ready. | "¿Tuvo oportunidad de practicar en un entorno real o simulado antes de que el cambio sea obligatorio en su día a día?" |
| **Reinforcement** | Mechanisms exist to make the change stick — recognition, corrective feedback, updated KPIs/incentives, visible leadership follow-through. | Change is declared "done" at go-live and nobody reinforces it afterward; old process still tacitly tolerated; no metric tracks adoption. | "¿Qué pasa hoy, tres meses después del go-live, si alguien vuelve a la forma vieja de trabajar?" |

**Sequencing rule**: ADKAR is cumulative and ordered — a gap in an earlier dimension blocks progress in the later ones no matter how well the later ones are executed (e.g. excellent training doesn't fix an Awareness or Desire gap). Always diagnose in order: Awareness → Desire → Knowledge → Ability → Reinforcement, and find the *first* one that's weak — that's the barrier point, and it's where the coaching effort should focus first.

**Pragmatic tactics per gap** (use these as the seed for concrete recommendations, adapted to the specific situation described):
- **Awareness gap** → 1:1 or small-group conversation (not another mass email) explaining the specific "why now" and what happens if nothing changes; make the sponsor visible, not just Comms/HR.
- **Desire gap** → surface the personal impact explicitly (workload, role, career, day-to-day friction removed or added); involve the resistant person/team in shaping *how* the change is implemented, not just receiving it; address past change fatigue directly instead of ignoring it.
- **Knowledge gap** → role-specific training (not generic), timed close to go-live, with job aids/quick reference material, not just a one-time session.
- **Ability gap** → sandbox/practice time under realistic conditions, on-the-job coaching or a super-user/buddy system in the first weeks, and confirmation the tools/systems are actually ready (an Ability gap is sometimes disguised as a "people" problem when it's actually a systems-readiness problem).
- **Reinforcement gap** → define what gets measured and reviewed after go-live, recognize early adopters visibly, have people managers actively follow up (not just a survey), and retire/disable the old way of working where feasible so it's not a standing alternative.

## Kotter's 8-Step Process

| # | Step | What it means | Common pitfall | Pragmatic diagnostic question |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Create urgency** | Build a compelling, shared sense that change is necessary now. | Urgency is asserted top-down ("leadership says so") instead of grounded in a real, felt problem; too much comfort/complacency in the org. | "¿Qué pasa concretamente si este cambio no se hace este año? ¿La gente lo siente o solo lo escuchó?" |
| 2 | **Build a guiding coalition** | Assemble a group with enough authority, credibility, and diversity to lead the change. | The coalition is only senior sponsors with no informal leaders/influencers; or it's one person carrying it alone. | "¿Quién en la organización, más allá del sponsor formal, tiene credibilidad para que la gente confíe en este cambio?" |
| 3 | **Form a strategic vision** | A clear, simple picture of the future state that's easy to communicate and rally around. | The vision is a slide full of jargon/strategy-speak that nobody can repeat in one sentence. | "¿Podés resumir el estado futuro en una frase que cualquier persona del equipo pueda repetir?" |
| 4 | **Enlist a volunteer army** | Communicate the vision broadly enough that people opt in, beyond the core team. | Communication is one-way and one-time; no channel for people to ask questions or push back. | "¿La gente se está sumando porque quiere, o porque se lo están imponiendo?" |
| 5 | **Enable action by removing barriers** | Clear structural/process/systems obstacles that block people from acting on the vision. | Barriers are known but nobody owns removing them (an outdated process, a system that doesn't support the new way, a manager who blocks it). | "¿Qué obstáculo concreto —proceso, sistema, política, persona— le impide hoy a la gente actuar distinto?" |
| 6 | **Generate short-term wins** | Deliver visible, unambiguous wins early to build momentum and credibility. | Wins are too far out (all benefit promised at the end) or too ambiguous to be recognized as a win. | "¿Cuál sería la primera victoria visible, en semanas, no en meses, que la gente pueda ver y celebrar?" |
| 7 | **Sustain acceleration** | Use early credibility to tackle bigger, harder changes instead of declaring victory too soon. | Momentum is lost celebrating step 6 as "done" instead of using it to push the next, harder wave of change. | "Después de la primera victoria, ¿qué es lo más difícil que todavía falta cambiar, y se está encarando o se relajó el esfuerzo?" |
| 8 | **Institute change** | Anchor new behaviors into culture, systems, and norms so they outlast the original change effort. | Nothing formally replaces the old KPIs/process/policy, so the change quietly reverts once attention moves elsewhere. | "Si el sponsor y el equipo de cambio se fueran mañana, ¿el cambio se sostiene solo?" |

**Sequencing rule**: Kotter's steps build on each other but, unlike ADKAR, real initiatives often need to revisit earlier steps (e.g. urgency fades and needs reinforcing in step 7). When coaching, identify which step the person is *actually* stuck on — it's common for someone to think they're stuck on step 6 ("we need more wins") when the real issue is step 2 or 5 (no real coalition, or a barrier nobody removed).

## Coaching approach for this skill

- Don't lecture the theory unprompted — the person came with a real situation or question; use the frameworks as a diagnostic lens, not a slide deck to present.
- Always translate the diagnosis into 2-4 concrete, situation-specific next actions, not a restated definition of the dimension/step.
- If the user's situation clearly spans both models (e.g. "el equipo de liderazgo ya definió la visión pero la gente en planta no se está sumando"), say so explicitly: name the Kotter step in play (Enlist a volunteer army) and the likely ADKAR gap underneath it (Desire, or Awareness) driving the resistance.
